20 Best Places to Visit in Indore for College Students (2026)

Your ultimate guide to exploring Indore on a student budget — historical sites, street food lanes, waterfalls, parks, and malls.

March 15, 2026 12 min read

Indore is not just the commercial capital of Madhya Pradesh — it is also a vibrant city full of history, culture, street food, and natural beauty. If you are a college student studying in Indore, you are in for a treat. From stunning heritage palaces to buzzing night markets, Indore has something for every budget and mood.

Whether you are at Medicaps University, IPS Academy, SD Bansal College, or any other institution in the city, weekends are your chance to explore. Here are the 20 best places to visit in Indore that every college student should check out.

1. Rajwada Palace — The Heart of Indore

Rajwada is a 200-year-old historic palace built by the Holkar dynasty. Located in the center of the city, this seven-story structure is a magnificent blend of Maratha, Mughal, and French architecture. The palace overlooks a bustling market area and is surrounded by traditional shops selling everything from bangles to spices.

Entry Fee: Free | Distance from Rau: ~14 km | Timings: 10 AM - 5 PM | Best for: History lovers, photography

2. Sarafa Bazaar — India's Famous Night Street Food Market

Sarafa Bazaar is a jewellery market by day and transforms into Indore's most iconic street food destination after 8 PM. This is where you will find the legendary garadu, bhutte ka kees, malpua, jalebi, and dozens of other dishes. It is a must-visit for any foodie student. Go with friends and eat your way through the lane — you can fill up for under Rs 200.

Entry Fee: Free | Distance from Rau: ~13 km | Timings: 8 PM - 2 AM | Budget: Rs 100-300 for food

3. Patalpani Waterfall — A Stunning Day Trip

Patalpani is a breathtaking 300-foot waterfall located about 35 km from Indore. During the monsoon season (July to October), the waterfall is at its most spectacular. The surrounding lush green hills and the railway bridge nearby add to the scenic beauty. It is a perfect spot for a group trip with college friends. You can reach there by bike or shared cab.

Entry Fee: Rs 20 | Distance from Rau: ~30 km | Best Season: July - October | Best for: Trekking, photography, picnics

4. Ralamandal Wildlife Sanctuary — Nature Close to Campus

Ralamandal is one of the oldest wildlife sanctuaries in India and is surprisingly close to the Rau area. It is perfect for a quick morning trek or an evening walk. The sanctuary is home to deer, peacocks, and various bird species. The trek to the hilltop offers a panoramic view of Indore city. It takes about 45 minutes to reach the top and is a fantastic workout.

Entry Fee: Rs 10 | Distance from Rau: ~8 km | Timings: 6 AM - 6 PM | Best for: Trekking, nature walks, bird watching

5. Chappan Dukan (56 Shops) — Street Food Heaven

Chappan Dukan is a collection of 56 food shops lined up in a row on Sapna-Sangeeta Road. From poha-jalebi in the morning to pani puri and momos in the evening, this place has it all. Johny Hot Dog and Vijay Chaat House are legendary names here. It is a great hangout for college groups any day of the week and is extremely pocket-friendly.

Entry Fee: Free | Distance from Rau: ~12 km | Timings: 8 AM - 11 PM | Budget: Rs 50-200

6. C21 Mall & Treasure Island Mall — Shopping & Movies

When you need a break from studies, head to C21 Mall or Treasure Island Mall for movies, gaming zones, and food courts. C21 Mall on AB Road is one of the biggest malls in central India with a PVR multiplex, gaming arcades, and brand stores. Treasure Island Mall near Palasia is another popular hangout with similar entertainment options. Both malls offer student-friendly pricing on weekdays.

Entry Fee: Free (movie tickets from Rs 150) | Distance from Rau: ~10-15 km | Best for: Movies, shopping, hanging out

7. Lal Bagh Palace — Royal Heritage

Lal Bagh Palace is a stunning example of European architecture in the heart of India. Built by the Holkar rulers over three generations, this palace features Italian marble floors, Persian carpets, Bohemian chandeliers, and a gate modelled on the Buckingham Palace gates. The sprawling lawns and the museum inside make it a perfect blend of education and leisure for students.

Entry Fee: Rs 10 (students) | Distance from Rau: ~15 km | Timings: 10 AM - 5 PM (closed Mondays) | Best for: History, architecture, photography

8. Meghdoot Garden & Nehru Park — Green Getaways

For students who love green spaces, Meghdoot Garden and Nehru Park are ideal. Meghdoot Garden near Race Course Road features musical fountains, boating, and well-maintained lawns. Nehru Park (Bal Vatika) is great for morning jogs and peaceful study sessions outdoors. Both parks offer affordable entry and are perfect for unwinding after a tough exam week.

Entry Fee: Rs 10-20 | Distance from Rau: ~10 km | Best for: Relaxation, morning walks, group hangouts

9. Annapurna Temple — Spiritual Serenity

Annapurna Temple is a beautiful replica of the Meenakshi Temple of Madurai. This temple is dedicated to Goddess Annapurna and features intricate Dravidian architecture with four ornate gopurams. The peaceful environment inside is a welcome break from the chaos of city life. Many students visit here for blessings before exams.

Entry Fee: Free | Distance from Rau: ~12 km | Timings: 5 AM - 9 PM | Best for: Spiritual peace, architecture

10. Pipliyapala Regional Park (Talawali Jheel)

This beautiful lakeside park is one of Indore's newest attractions. Spread across 80 acres, it features a musical fountain, boating, food court, and a long walking track around the lake. The evenings here are especially beautiful with the fountain show. It is an excellent spot for a budget-friendly outing with friends.

Entry Fee: Rs 20 | Distance from Rau: ~16 km | Timings: 10 AM - 9 PM | Best for: Evening hangouts, photography, boating

11-15. More Hidden Gems for Students

11. Kanch Mandir (Glass Temple): A unique Jain temple made entirely of glass and mirrors. Entry is free, located in the city center, about 14 km from Rau. A truly one-of-a-kind experience.

12. Central Museum (Indore Museum): Home to rare Parmar-era sculptures and a collection of medieval artefacts. Entry is just Rs 10 for students. Located near GPO, about 13 km from Rau.

13. Khajrana Ganesh Temple: One of the most visited temples in Indore, known for wish fulfilment. The market outside is great for shopping. About 18 km from Rau, with no entry fee.

14. Gommatgiri: A peaceful Jain pilgrimage site featuring a 21-foot statue of Lord Bahubali. Located about 10 km from Rau, it offers stunning panoramic views of Indore from the hilltop. Free entry.

15. Mahatma Gandhi Hall (Town Hall): A stunning Indo-Gothic building built in 1904. The clock tower is iconic to Indore's skyline. Located centrally, about 13 km from Rau. A must-see for architecture enthusiasts.

16-20. Adventure, Cafes & More

16. Tincha Falls: Another spectacular waterfall near Indore (about 25 km from Rau). Best visited during monsoons. Great for a group trek with college friends. Entry is Rs 20.

17. Phoenix Citadel Mall: The newest and biggest mall in Indore with international brands, IMAX theatre, and a massive food court. About 8 km from Rau. Perfect for weekend movie plans.

18. Cafe Culture on MG Road: Indore has a booming cafe scene. Try cafes like The Chalkboard, Books & Brews, or Cafe Terazza on MG Road. Budget Rs 150-300 per person. Great for study sessions and group meetups.

19. Kam Biyabani (Deer Park): A small but lovely deer park and garden near Rau itself, just about 5 km away. Ideal for a quick evening escape with zero planning needed.

20. Bijasen Tekri: A hilltop temple offering breathtaking sunset views over Indore. About 18 km from Rau, with free entry. The panoramic cityscape at golden hour is Instagram-worthy.

Pro Tips for Student Explorers

  • Travel in groups of 4-5 to split auto and cab fares — a ride from Rau to city center costs Rs 30-50 per person by shared auto.
  • Carry your college ID — many heritage sites and museums offer discounted entry for students.
  • Visit Sarafa Bazaar on weekdays to avoid the weekend crowd.
  • Monsoon season (July-September) is the best time for waterfalls like Patalpani and Tincha.
  • Use Ola/Uber or Indore city buses (AICTSL) for affordable transportation across the city.
