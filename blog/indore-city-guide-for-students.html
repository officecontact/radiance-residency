Indore City Guide for Students — Everything You Need to Know (2026)

March 15, 2026 8 min read

Moving to a new city for college is both exciting and overwhelming. If you are headed to Indore for your higher education, congratulations — you have picked one of India's finest cities for students. Indore, the commercial capital of Madhya Pradesh, has consistently been ranked the cleanest city in India for multiple years. It is also known as the food capital of India, offering an incredible street food culture that will keep your taste buds happy throughout your college years.

Whether you are joining Medicaps University, IPS Academy, IIM Indore, or any other institution in the Rau-Indore belt, this guide will help you understand everything about the city before you arrive. From transport options and weather patterns to the cost of living and safety, we have covered it all.

Why Indore Is the Best City for Students

Indore has emerged as a major education hub in central India, and for good reason. The city hosts over 50 engineering colleges, management institutes, medical colleges, and universities. Here is what makes it stand out:

India's Cleanest City: Indore has won the Swachh Survekshan award multiple times in a row, making it the cleanest city in India. Clean roads, well-maintained public spaces, and excellent waste management mean you will be living in a hygienic, well-organized environment. For students coming from smaller towns, this is a significant comfort factor.

The Food Capital: Indore is famous across India for its street food. From the iconic Sarafa Bazaar night market to Chappan Dukan (56 shops), you will find an unbelievable variety of poha-jalebi, bhutte ka kees, garadu, sabudana khichdi, dal bafla, and much more. The best part? Street food in Indore is incredibly affordable, typically costing just 20 to 80 rupees per plate. As a student, you will never run out of affordable and delicious food options.

Growing IT and Startup Hub: Indore is rapidly developing its IT sector with the Super Corridor hosting offices of major companies like TCS, Infosys, and Cognizant. For engineering and management students, this means internship and placement opportunities are growing every year without having to relocate to Bangalore or Pune.

Getting Around Indore — Transport Guide

By Air: Devi Ahilya Bai Holkar Airport connects Indore to all major Indian cities including Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, and Kolkata. Airlines like IndiGo, SpiceJet, Air India, and Vistara operate daily flights. The airport is about 8 kilometers from the city center, and you can easily reach your college area by cab or auto.

By Train: Indore Junction is well-connected to cities across India. Regular trains run to Delhi, Mumbai, Jaipur, Bhopal, and more. For students from Rajasthan, Gujarat, and Maharashtra, trains are often the most affordable option. The Rau area (where Medicaps University is located) is about 12 kilometers from Indore Junction.

By Road: Indore is connected by national highways to Bhopal (190 km), Ujjain (55 km), Ahmedabad (400 km), and Mumbai (585 km). Private bus operators like Neeta, Hans, and Konduskar run comfortable AC buses to and from most cities. The Gangwal Bus Stand is the main bus terminal in Indore.

Local Transport: Within Indore, you can use auto-rickshaws, city buses (iBus / AICTSL), Ola, Uber, and Rapido for daily commuting. Most auto rides within the city cost between 30 and 100 rupees. If you live near your college — like at Radiance Residency, which is directly opposite Medicaps University — you can save significantly on daily transport costs by simply walking to class.

Indore Weather — What to Expect

Indore has a tropical climate with three distinct seasons. Summers (March to June) can get quite hot, with temperatures reaching 40 to 44 degrees Celsius in May and June. An AC room is highly recommended during this period. Monsoons (July to September) bring pleasant rainfall and temperatures drop to a comfortable 25 to 30 degrees. Keep an umbrella and waterproof shoes handy. Winters (November to February) are mild and pleasant, with temperatures ranging from 8 to 25 degrees Celsius. Nights can get chilly in December and January, so pack some warm clothes.

Culture and Student Life in Indore

Indore is a vibrant, welcoming city with a mix of traditional Malwa culture and modern urban lifestyle. The people of Indore are known for their warmth and hospitality. Hindi is the primary language, but you will find that English is widely understood, especially in educational and commercial areas. Students from all over India — Rajasthan, Gujarat, Maharashtra, UP, Bihar, and even South India — come to Indore for education, creating a diverse and multicultural student community.

For entertainment, Indore offers multiplexes like PVR and INOX at malls such as Treasure Island, Phoenix Citadel, and C21 Mall. There are numerous cafes, restaurants, and hangout spots popular among students. Rajwada Palace, Lal Bagh Palace, and Patalpani Waterfall are must-visit weekend destinations. The city also hosts cultural festivals, college fests, and tech events throughout the year.

Cost of Living for Students in Indore

One of the biggest advantages of studying in Indore compared to cities like Pune, Bangalore, or Delhi is the significantly lower cost of living. Here is a rough monthly breakdown for a student:

  • Accommodation: Hostel rooms range from 5,000 to 12,000 rupees per month depending on amenities. At Radiance Residency, all-inclusive rooms start at just 7,000 rupees per month with meals, Wi-Fi, and all utilities included.
  • Food: If your hostel includes meals, you save a lot. Otherwise, budget about 3,000 to 5,000 rupees for meals outside. Street food can cost as little as 30 to 50 rupees per meal.
  • Transport: If you live near your college, transport costs are minimal. Otherwise, budget 500 to 1,500 rupees monthly for auto or bus rides.
  • Entertainment and Miscellaneous: Around 1,000 to 3,000 rupees for outings, recharges, stationery, and personal expenses.
  • Total Estimate: A student can comfortably live in Indore for 10,000 to 20,000 rupees per month all-inclusive, which is far more affordable than most metro cities.

Safety in Indore

Indore is generally considered a safe city for students. The crime rate is relatively low compared to larger metros, and the city has an active police presence. Most college areas, including Rau, are residential and calm. That said, like any city, basic precautions are important: avoid walking alone late at night in unfamiliar areas, keep your belongings secure, and always share your live location with family when traveling. Choosing a hostel with 24/7 CCTV security and controlled access — like Radiance Residency — adds an extra layer of safety and peace of mind for both students and their parents.

The Rau Area — Education Hub

If you are studying at Medicaps University, SD Bansal College, IPS Academy, or IIST Indore, you will likely be based in the Rau area on the outskirts of Indore. Rau has developed significantly over the past decade and now has its own ecosystem of hostels, restaurants, stationery shops, ATMs, medical stores, and grocery shops catering specifically to students. The area is quieter and less congested than central Indore, making it ideal for focused academic life while still being well-connected to the city via the main Indore-Rau road.

Must-Visit Places in Indore

During your time in Indore, make sure to explore these popular spots: Sarafa Bazaar for late-night street food, Chappan Dukan for iconic Indori snacks, Rajwada Palace for historical architecture, Patalpani Waterfall for a monsoon day trip, Ralamandal Wildlife Sanctuary for trekking, and Treasure Island Mall for shopping and movies. Each of these is affordable and easily accessible for students.
