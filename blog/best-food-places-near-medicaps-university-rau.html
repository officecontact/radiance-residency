15 Best Food Places Near Medicaps University, Rau (2026)

March 10, 2026 10 min read

One of the best parts of college life is exploring local food joints with your friends. If you are studying at Medicaps University or any college in Rau, Indore, you are surrounded by dozens of affordable and delicious food options. From quick bites between classes to weekend dinner outings, this list covers the 15 best food places near Medicaps University that every student should try in 2026.

Note: Radiance Residency provides three home-style vegetarian meals daily (breakfast, lunch, and dinner) included in the rent. But these food spots are perfect for weekend outings, late-night cravings, and hangouts with friends.

Street Food & Quick Bites

1. Rau Chauraha Maggi & Chai Stalls

Famous for: Butter Maggi, Masala Chai, Bread Omelette

Price range: 20-50 rupees

The iconic Maggi and chai stalls near Rau Chauraha are a student institution. Whether it is a cold winter morning or a late-night study break, a plate of steaming butter Maggi and a cutting chai is the go-to comfort food for every Medicaps student. Multiple stalls compete here, and each has its own loyal fanbase.

2. Campus Gate Momos Wala

Famous for: Steamed Momos, Fried Momos, Tandoori Momos

Price range: 40-80 rupees

Right outside the Medicaps campus gate, you will find a popular momos cart that has become a post-class ritual for many students. The spicy red chutney paired with freshly steamed momos is absolutely addictive. The tandoori momos, although slightly pricier, are worth every rupee. Quick service makes it perfect for grabbing a bite between lectures.

3. Sharma Ji Ki Kachori

Famous for: Kachori, Samosa, Jalebi

Price range: 15-40 rupees

For authentic Indori breakfast vibes, this small shop near Rau market serves piping hot kachoris with tangy sev and chutney every morning. Students who wake up early enough often skip hostel breakfast just for this experience. The samosas are equally crispy and loaded with filling. Pair it with a hot glass of masala doodh for the perfect start to your day.

4. Sandwich Corner

Famous for: Grilled Sandwiches, Cheese Sandwich, Club Sandwich

Price range: 40-100 rupees

Located on the main road near Rau, this small eatery has built a reputation for its loaded grilled sandwiches. The cheese burst sandwich with extra mayonnaise is a student favourite. They also serve fresh fruit juices and shakes that are perfect for the scorching Indore summers. Fast preparation means you can grab and go between classes.

5. Poha & Jalebi Point

Famous for: Indori Poha, Jalebi, Garadu (seasonal)

Price range: 20-50 rupees

No Indore food guide is complete without mentioning Poha and Jalebi. This small stall near Pigdamber serves the classic Indori poha topped with sev, onion, and a squeeze of lemon. During winters, they also serve Garadu, a seasonal deep-fried yam snack that is an Indore specialty. A plate of poha with jalebi for under 40 rupees is the best breakfast deal in Rau.

Budget Restaurants & Thali Places

6. Shree Bhojanalaya

Famous for: Unlimited Veg Thali, Dal-Rice, Roti-Sabzi

Price range: 70-100 rupees

This no-frills thali restaurant is where budget-conscious students go for a filling meal. The unlimited veg thali includes dal, two sabzis, rice, roti, papad, and salad at a price that will not dent your monthly pocket money. The food is home-style and clean, making it a reliable daily option for students who do not have meals included in their accommodation.

7. South Indian Corner

Famous for: Masala Dosa, Idli-Sambhar, Uttapam

Price range: 50-120 rupees

Craving South Indian food in Rau? This small restaurant serves crispy dosas, fluffy idlis, and tangy sambhar that can rival many places in the city. The Masala Dosa is the star of the menu, served with coconut chutney and piping hot sambhar. It is a popular breakfast and evening snack spot among South Indian students who miss food from home.

8. Rajasthani Dhaba

Famous for: Dal Baati Churma, Gatte Ki Sabzi, Rajasthani Thali

Price range: 80-150 rupees

Located on Bypass Road near Rau, this dhaba-style restaurant brings the flavours of Rajasthan to Indore. The dal baati churma is their signature dish, prepared fresh and served with generous amounts of ghee. Students from Rajasthan find this place particularly comforting. The portions are generous, and the rustic ambience adds to the experience.

Cafes & Hangout Spots

9. The Study Cafe

Famous for: Cold Coffee, Pasta, Wi-Fi seating for study

Price range: 80-200 rupees

This cafe has become the unofficial study hall for Medicaps students. With decent Wi-Fi, comfortable seating, and a menu designed for long stays, it is the perfect place to work on group projects or prepare for exams. Their cold coffee is legendary among students, and the pasta is a reliable comfort food option. The ambience is cozy and Instagram-worthy.

10. Chai Sutta Bar

Famous for: Kulhad Chai, Bun Maska, Maggi

Price range: 30-100 rupees

Born in Indore and now a national chain, Chai Sutta Bar has outlets near the Rau area that are always buzzing with students. The kulhad chai (clay pot tea) is their signature offering, and the affordable pricing makes it the perfect spot for those long conversations with friends after college. The bun maska and Maggi are the most ordered sides.

11. Pizza Point

Famous for: Veg Pizza, Garlic Bread, Pasta

Price range: 100-250 rupees

When the pizza craving hits and Dominos feels too expensive, this local pizza joint is the answer. Their hand-tossed pizzas with generous cheese are a hit among students. The garlic bread with cheese dip is a must-try side. They also offer delivery to nearby hostels, making it a popular choice for late-night group study pizza parties.

Sweets & Desserts

12. Rau Ice Cream Parlour

Famous for: Gola, Ice Cream Sundaes, Faluda

Price range: 30-120 rupees

A summer essential for every student in Rau. This parlour serves a variety of ice cream flavours, thick shakes, and the classic Indori gola (flavoured ice). During peak summers when Indore temperatures cross 45 degrees, this place sees a constant stream of students cooling down with their favourite frozen treats.

13. Gupta Sweets

Famous for: Rabri, Gulab Jamun, Malpua, Namkeen

Price range: 30-100 rupees

For traditional Indian sweets and Indori namkeen, this sweet shop in Rau market is a trusted name. Students often pick up sweets from here during festivals or when celebrating exam results. Their rabri is thick and creamy, and the gulab jamuns are perfectly syrupy. They also stock a good selection of Indori namkeen snacks for those hostel room munchies.

Late Night Food Options

14. Night Chai & Egg Stall

Famous for: Egg Bhurji, Chai, Bread Toast

Price range: 20-60 rupees

When you are studying late into the night and need a pick-me-up, this stall near Rau Chauraha operates till around midnight. The egg bhurji with buttered bread is the perfect late-night fuel, and the strong chai will keep you awake for those last few chapters before an exam. It has become a bonding spot for students pulling all-nighters.

15. Bypass Road Food Trucks

Famous for: Paneer Tikka, Spring Rolls, Corn Chat

Price range: 50-150 rupees

The food trucks along Bypass Road come alive in the evening and are a popular weekend hangout for Rau students. The variety is impressive: from smoky paneer tikka to crispy spring rolls and loaded corn chat. Sitting outdoors with friends, sharing plates of food under the open sky, is one of those college memories you will cherish long after graduation.

Pro Tips for Students

  • Download Zomato and Swiggy for food delivery right to your hostel on lazy days.
  • Ask seniors for their favourite hidden food gems. Every batch has its own secret spots.
  • Consider a hostel with meals included to save money on daily food expenses. Use the savings for weekend outings.
  • Visit Indore's famous Sarafa Bazaar night market at least once. It opens at 8 PM and is worth the trip.
  • Keep some instant noodles and biscuits in your room for midnight hunger pangs.
