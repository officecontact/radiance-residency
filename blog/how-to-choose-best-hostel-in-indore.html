How to Choose the Best Hostel in Indore — 10 Things to Check Before Booking

March 5, 2026 9 min read

Choosing the right hostel or PG accommodation is one of the most important decisions you will make as a college student. The place you live directly impacts your academic performance, mental well-being, daily routine, and overall college experience. Yet, many students rush this decision and end up regretting it within weeks of moving in.

Whether you are heading to Medicaps University, IPS Academy, SD Bansal College, or any other institution in Indore, this comprehensive checklist of 10 things will help you evaluate and compare hostels like a pro. By the end of this guide, you will know exactly what to look for and what questions to ask before signing that booking form.

1. Location & Distance from College

This is arguably the most critical factor. The closer your hostel is to your college, the more time you save every single day. A hostel that is 30 minutes away by auto might not sound bad, but multiply that by two trips daily, six days a week, and nine months in a year. That adds up to hundreds of hours and thousands of rupees spent on commuting.

Ideally, your hostel should be within a 5-minute walk from campus. This allows you to go back to your room between classes, have lunch comfortably, and avoid the stress of being late due to traffic or transport delays. During exams, those extra 30 minutes of sleep instead of commuting can make a real difference.

What to ask: "How far is the hostel from my college? Can I walk there? How long does it take during peak hours?"

2. Food Quality & Meal Plan

Food is fuel, and as a student, you need proper nutrition to keep up with the demands of college life. Check whether the hostel provides meals and, if so, how many meals per day. Visit during meal time if possible and taste the food before booking. Ask current residents about the food quality, variety, and hygiene.

A hostel that provides three meals daily (breakfast, lunch, and dinner) saves you significant time and money compared to eating out every day. Calculate the actual cost: eating three meals outside can easily cost 200-300 rupees per day, which is 6,000-9,000 rupees per month just for food. A hostel with meals included in the rent often works out much cheaper.

What to ask: "Are meals included? How many meals per day? Is it vegetarian or non-vegetarian? Can I see the kitchen and taste the food?"

3. Safety & Security

Your parents are trusting a hostel to keep you safe, and this is not something to compromise on. Look for hostels with CCTV cameras installed in common areas and entry points, a dedicated security guard or staff, and controlled access so that outsiders cannot walk in freely.

Ask about the hostel's visitor policy, curfew timings, and emergency protocols. A well-managed hostel will have clear rules about visitors, late entry, and will have contact information for the owner or manager who can be reached in emergencies. Also check if the building has proper fire safety measures like extinguishers and emergency exits.

What to ask: "Is there CCTV? Is there a security guard? What is the visitor policy? What are the entry/exit timings?"

4. Cleanliness & Hygiene

A dirty hostel is not just unpleasant; it is a health hazard. When you visit a hostel, pay close attention to the condition of the bathrooms, common areas, kitchen, and rooms. Are the bathrooms clean? Is there regular housekeeping? Are the corridors and stairs well maintained? Is there proper waste disposal?

Ask how often rooms and common areas are cleaned. Daily housekeeping is ideal. Some hostels charge extra for cleaning, while others include it in the rent. Also check the water quality: does the hostel provide RO purified drinking water? Is there hot water for bathing during winters?

What to ask: "How often is cleaning done? Is there daily housekeeping? Is RO water available? Is there hot water in winters?"

5. Wi-Fi & Internet Speed

In 2026, reliable internet is not a luxury; it is a necessity for students. Online assignments, lecture recordings, research, video calls with family, and even entertainment — everything depends on good Wi-Fi. Ask about the internet speed and whether it is included in the rent or charged separately.

Test the Wi-Fi when you visit. Open a video on YouTube and check if it streams smoothly. Ask current residents about the reliability: does it go down frequently? Is the speed consistent across all rooms? Some hostels have Wi-Fi only in common areas, which is not ideal if you need to work from your room late at night.

What to ask: "What is the Wi-Fi speed? Is it included in rent? Does it work in all rooms? Is it reliable during peak hours?"

6. Room Quality & Furnishing

The room is where you will spend most of your non-college hours, so it needs to be comfortable. Check whether the room comes with a proper bed, mattress, study table, chair, wardrobe, and adequate storage. Look at the ventilation: is there a window? Is there a fan or AC option?

Also check the bathroom situation: is it attached or shared? Attached bathrooms offer significantly more convenience and privacy. Inspect the condition of the bathroom fixtures, water pressure, and drainage. If the hostel offers both AC and non-AC rooms, consider your budget and Indore's hot summers, which can make non-AC rooms quite uncomfortable from March to June.

7. Transparent Pricing — No Hidden Charges

One of the biggest complaints students have about hostels is hidden charges that appear after they move in. The rent advertised might be just the base rate, with electricity, water, Wi-Fi, maintenance, and meals all charged separately. What seemed like a 5,000 rupee room suddenly becomes 10,000 when you add everything up.

Before booking, get a clear written breakdown of what is included in the rent and what is extra. Ask about security deposits and refund policies. The best hostels offer all-inclusive pricing where meals, Wi-Fi, electricity, housekeeping, and all amenities are included in one fixed monthly fee with zero surprises.

What to ask: "What exactly is included in the rent? Are there any additional monthly charges? What is the security deposit and is it refundable?"

8. Power Backup

Indore experiences power cuts, especially during summers. Imagine being in the middle of an online exam or assignment submission when the power goes out. A hostel with generator or inverter backup ensures uninterrupted electricity for lights, fans, and Wi-Fi routers. Ask specifically how quickly the backup kicks in and how long it can sustain power.

9. Reviews & Reputation

Always check what current and former residents say about a hostel. Search for Google reviews, ask your college seniors, and visit student forums and WhatsApp groups. Real student experiences give you insights that no brochure or website can provide. Pay attention to recurring complaints; if multiple people mention the same issue, it is likely genuine.

If possible, visit the hostel and speak with current residents directly. Ask them what they like and dislike about the place. Their honest feedback is invaluable. A hostel with consistently positive reviews and a good reputation among students is almost always a safe choice.

10. Warden/Management Responsiveness

The quality of the hostel management makes a huge difference in your daily experience. Are they responsive when there is a maintenance issue? Do they address complaints promptly? Is the warden or manager approachable and available? How do they handle emergencies?

A well-managed hostel will have clear communication channels, responsive staff, and a proactive approach to maintaining the property. If the hostel takes days to fix a leaking tap or broken fan, imagine living there for an entire academic year. Gauge the management's attitude during your initial visit and inquiry calls.

Quick Comparison Checklist

Use this checklist when visiting hostels. Score each factor out of 10:

  • Location and distance from college
  • Food quality and meal plan
  • Safety and security measures
  • Cleanliness and hygiene standards
  • Wi-Fi speed and reliability
  • Room quality and furnishing
  • Transparent and all-inclusive pricing
  • Power backup availability
  • Reviews and reputation
  • Management responsiveness
