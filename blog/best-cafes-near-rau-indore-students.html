10 Best Cafes Near Rau, Indore for Students — Study & Hangout Spots

Your ultimate guide to the best cafes for studying, group projects, and hanging out near Rau and Indore.

March 15, 2026 8 min read Radiance Residency

If you are a student in Rau or Indore, you know the struggle — finding a good cafe where you can study in peace, work on group projects, or simply unwind after a long day of lectures. The Rau-Indore corridor, especially around Medicaps University, IPS Academy, and SD Bansal College, has seen a surge of student-friendly cafes in recent years.

We have put together this curated list of the 10 best cafes near Rau, Indore that are perfect for students — whether you need Wi-Fi for assignments, a quiet corner for exam prep, or a fun spot for weekend hangouts with friends.

1. Cafe Jeune — The All-Rounder

Location: Vijay Nagar, Indore (20 min from Rau)

Average cost: ₹200-400 for two

Cafe Jeune is a favourite among Indore's college crowd. With its spacious interiors, reliable Wi-Fi, and plenty of charging points, it is an ideal spot for long study sessions. Their cold coffee and sandwiches are budget-friendly. The ambient lighting and comfortable seating make it easy to spend hours here without feeling rushed. They also have a separate quiet zone upstairs, which is perfect for focused studying.

2. The Brew Estate

Location: Bypass Road, near Rau

Average cost: ₹300-500 for two

A trendy spot with excellent coffee and a cozy atmosphere. The Brew Estate offers free high-speed Wi-Fi and has comfortable booth seating, making it great for group project discussions. Their continental menu has plenty of affordable options for students. On weekends, they sometimes host open mic nights, which makes it a fun hangout destination too.

3. Cafe Terazza

Location: Rau Main Road

Average cost: ₹150-300 for two

One of the closest cafes to Medicaps University, Cafe Terazza is a go-to for students who want a quick coffee break between classes. The prices are very student-friendly, and they have a rooftop section that is especially pleasant in the evenings. Their masala chai and maggi combo is legendary among the local student community. Wi-Fi is available, though it can get slow during peak hours.

4. Mocha — The Study Cafe

Location: Pigdamber Road, Rau

Average cost: ₹150-250 for two

Mocha is practically designed for students. With its quiet environment, strong Wi-Fi, and an explicit "no loud music" policy during weekday afternoons, it is a haven for exam preparation. Their menu is budget-conscious with items starting at just ₹50. The staff are understanding about students sitting for long hours, which is a big plus. Multiple power outlets at every table ensure your laptop never dies mid-assignment.

5. Hashtag Cafe

Location: Near IPS Academy, Rau-Pithampur Road

Average cost: ₹200-350 for two

Known for its Instagram-worthy interiors and lively vibe, Hashtag Cafe is where you go when you want to celebrate after exams or catch up with friends. They have board games, a pool table, and a DJ on weekends. The food is decent and reasonably priced, with their pasta and shakes being the most popular items. Not the best for quiet studying, but fantastic for group hangouts and celebrations.

6. Chapter One Cafe

Location: Scheme 78, Vijay Nagar

Average cost: ₹250-400 for two

A book-themed cafe that is a paradise for students who love reading and studying in a literary atmosphere. The shelves are lined with books you can borrow while you sit. Their filter coffee is exceptional, and they offer a student discount of 10% on weekdays. The Wi-Fi is fast and reliable, and the cafe stays open until 11 PM, making it great for those late-night study marathons before exams.

7. Bake N Shake

Location: Rau Square

Average cost: ₹100-200 for two

The most budget-friendly option on this list. Bake N Shake is perfect for students on a tight budget who just need a place to sit with a coffee and work on their laptop. Their milkshakes are a steal at ₹60, and the baked goods are freshly made. The space is small but cozy, and the owners are known for being very welcoming to students. Basic Wi-Fi is available, and the location right at Rau Square makes it very accessible.

8. The White Owl

Location: Bypass Road, Indore

Average cost: ₹300-500 for two

A slightly upscale cafe that is worth the splurge for special occasions. The White Owl has a beautiful garden seating area and serves some of the best European-style coffee in Indore. They offer dedicated "study tables" with power outlets and strong Wi-Fi. Their weekend brunch is popular, and they have a student loyalty card — buy 9 coffees, get the 10th free. The ambiance is calm and sophisticated.

9. Chai Sutta Bar

Location: Multiple outlets in Rau and Indore

Average cost: ₹80-150 for two

Born in Indore, Chai Sutta Bar has become a nationwide phenomenon, and for good reason. Their kulhad chai is iconic, and the prices are incredibly student-friendly. The Rau outlet near Medicaps University is always buzzing with students. While it is not the quietest place for deep studying, it is perfect for quick chai breaks, casual catch-ups, and light discussions. Their maggi and sandwiches are filling and cheap.

10. Cafe Jeera

Location: Near SD Bansal College, Rau

Average cost: ₹150-300 for two

A hidden gem near SD Bansal College, Cafe Jeera blends Indian and continental flavours with a homely atmosphere. They have a dedicated study area on the first floor with fast Wi-Fi and minimal distractions. Their thali meals are a great lunch option for students who want something more filling than snacks. The cafe often hosts quiz nights and poetry readings that attract the creative student crowd.

Tips for Studying at Cafes

Before heading out to your favourite cafe, keep these tips in mind to make the most of your study session:

  • Go during off-peak hours: Weekday mornings (10 AM - 12 PM) are the quietest. Avoid weekends if you need to focus.
  • Carry your charger and earphones: Even cafes with power outlets may have limited sockets. Noise-cancelling earphones are a game-changer.
  • Order something every 2 hours: It is polite and ensures you stay welcome for long sessions. A simple chai or coffee will do.
  • Use a hotspot as backup: Cafe Wi-Fi can be unreliable during peak hours. Keep your mobile data ready.
  • Study in groups wisely: Pick a larger cafe like Cafe Jeune or The Brew Estate for group study. Smaller cafes work better solo.
