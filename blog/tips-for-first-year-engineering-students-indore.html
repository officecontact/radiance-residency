15 Tips for First-Year Engineering Students in Indore (2026)

March 10, 2026 10 min read

Starting your engineering journey is one of the most transformative experiences of your life. The first year sets the tone for everything that follows — your academic performance, your friendships, your career trajectory, and even your personal growth. If you are about to begin your B.Tech or B.E. at a college in Indore like Medicaps University, IPS Academy, or IIST, this guide is specifically for you.

We have compiled 15 practical, actionable tips that will help you navigate your first year with confidence. These are not generic advice — they are based on real experiences of students who have lived and studied in Indore.

Academic Tips

1. Attend Every Class in the First Semester: It might be tempting to skip lectures, especially when you feel the professor is boring or the subject seems easy. Resist the urge. Attendance matters for internal marks, and more importantly, the first semester builds foundational concepts in mathematics, physics, and basic engineering that you will need throughout your degree. Many students who skip classes in the first semester struggle to catch up later.

2. Make Notes by Hand: Studies consistently show that handwriting notes helps you retain information better than typing or photographing slides. Carry a notebook to every class and write down key points in your own words. These notes will be invaluable during exam preparation. You can digitize them later using apps like CamScanner if you want backups.

3. Start Exam Preparation Early: Do not wait until the last two weeks before exams to start studying. Engineering subjects build on each other, and cramming does not work for subjects like engineering mathematics, physics, or programming. Ideally, revise each topic within 48 hours of the lecture. Even 30 minutes of daily revision will put you far ahead of most of your peers.

4. Use YouTube and Online Resources: If you do not understand a concept in class, do not just let it go. YouTube channels like Neso Academy, Gate Smashers, and NPTEL lectures are excellent supplementary resources. Platforms like GeeksforGeeks, Tutorialspoint, and W3Schools are great for programming subjects. The key is to clarify doubts the same day rather than letting them pile up.

5. Build a Strong Foundation in Programming: Regardless of your branch — CSE, IT, Mechanical, Civil, or Electronics — basic programming skills are essential in 2026. Start with C programming, then move to Python or Java. Practice on platforms like HackerRank, LeetCode, or CodeChef. Even 30 minutes of coding practice daily will put you miles ahead by the time placement season arrives in your third year.

Hostel Life Tips

6. Choose Your Accommodation Wisely: Where you live has a massive impact on your academic performance and overall well-being. Choose a hostel that is close to your college to save commute time, offers proper meals so you do not rely on junk food, has reliable Wi-Fi for online resources, and provides a quiet study environment. A hostel like Radiance Residency, located directly opposite Medicaps University, checks all these boxes with rooms starting at just 7,000 rupees per month including meals and all amenities.

7. Establish a Daily Routine: Freedom is exciting, but it can also be your downfall if not managed well. Set a consistent wake-up time, meal times, study hours, and bedtime. Your body and brain perform better with routine. Include at least 6 to 7 hours of study time (including classes) and 7 to 8 hours of sleep in your daily schedule. Leave room for socializing and entertainment too — balance is key.

8. Learn to Do Your Own Laundry and Basic Chores: If this is your first time living away from home, basic life skills become essential. Learn to wash clothes, keep your room clean, manage your belongings, and store food properly. These skills might seem trivial, but they contribute significantly to your comfort and hygiene. If your hostel provides daily housekeeping — like Radiance Residency does — that is a huge advantage, but you should still maintain personal organization.

9. Stay Hydrated and Eat Well: Engineering students often survive on Maggi, chips, and cold drinks — this is a terrible habit that leads to low energy, poor concentration, and health problems. Make sure you eat three proper meals a day. If your hostel provides home-style meals, take full advantage of it. Drink plenty of water, especially during Indore's hot summers. Keep fruits and dry snacks in your room for when you study late.

10. Keep Your Valuables Secure: Always lock your room when you leave, even for just a few minutes. Keep your laptop, phone, wallet, and important documents in secure places. Avoid flaunting expensive gadgets. It is also wise to keep a digital copy of all important documents — Aadhaar card, college ID, hostel receipt — on your phone and cloud storage.

Social and Career Tips

11. Make Friends Across Branches: Do not limit your social circle to just your branch or section. Engineering college is one of the few places where you will meet people from all over India with diverse backgrounds and perspectives. Join college clubs, attend fests, participate in hackathons, and engage in extracurricular activities. The friendships you build here often last a lifetime and can be professionally valuable too.

12. Join at Least One Technical Club: Most engineering colleges in Indore have technical clubs for coding, robotics, IoT, design, or entrepreneurship. Joining one gives you hands-on experience, mentorship from seniors, and exposure to real-world projects. This practical experience is often more valuable than classroom learning and looks great on your resume when you start applying for internships.

13. Start Building Your LinkedIn Profile Early: Do not wait until final year to create a LinkedIn profile. Set it up in your first year itself. Connect with seniors, alumni, professors, and industry professionals. Share your projects, certifications, and learnings. A well-maintained LinkedIn profile with consistent activity over four years is far more impressive to recruiters than one hastily created before placements.

14. Learn at Least One Skill Outside Your Curriculum: The engineering curriculum alone will not make you job-ready. Pick one skill that interests you and invest time in it — web development, app development, data science, graphic design, video editing, digital marketing, or even content writing. Online platforms like Coursera, Udemy, and freeCodeCamp offer affordable and often free courses. This additional skill can be your differentiator during placements.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

15. Avoid These First-Year Traps: Here are the most common mistakes first-year engineering students make, based on what seniors and alumni have shared:

  • Thinking first year does not matter: Your first-year GPA is part of your cumulative CGPA. Companies do look at your aggregate marks during placements. A poor first year can drag down your overall score.
  • Excessive gaming or social media: It is easy to lose hours every day to mobile games, Instagram reels, or YouTube shorts. Set screen time limits and use apps like Digital Wellbeing to track usage.
  • Not asking for help: If you are struggling with a subject, a concept, or even personal issues, do not suffer in silence. Talk to your classmates, seniors, professors, or hostel friends. Everyone goes through tough patches — reaching out is a sign of maturity, not weakness.
  • Ignoring physical and mental health: College stress is real. Make time for exercise, whether it is a morning walk, gym session, or playing cricket. If you feel anxious or overwhelmed, talk to someone you trust. Many colleges have counselling services available.
  • Spending recklessly: The sudden freedom with money can lead to overspending on food deliveries, online shopping, and outings. Track your expenses from day one and set a monthly budget with your parents.
