Student Life in Rau, Indore — The Ultimate Guide (2026)

March 15, 2026 8 min read

If you are about to start college at Medicaps University, IPS Academy, SD Bansal College, or any other institution in the Rau area, you are about to enter one of the fastest-growing student hubs in Central India. Rau is a thriving suburb of Indore, and thousands of students from across Madhya Pradesh and neighbouring states call this place home during their college years. This guide covers everything you need to know about living in Rau as a student in 2026.

Why Rau Is Becoming a Major Student Hub

Rau sits along the Indore-Dhar highway, approximately 15 kilometres from the heart of Indore city. Over the past decade, Rau has transformed from a quiet town into a vibrant educational hub. The presence of major institutions like Medicaps University, IPS Academy, IIST, SD Bansal College, and the proximity to IIM Indore has attracted a growing student population every year.

What makes Rau particularly appealing is its affordability compared to central Indore. Rent, food, and daily expenses are significantly lower, yet the area has all modern conveniences. The neighbourhood has experienced rapid development with new restaurants, cafes, stationery shops, gyms, and grocery stores popping up to cater to the student community.

The peaceful environment, away from the noise and congestion of the main city, is ideal for academic focus. Students get the best of both worlds: a calm study environment in Rau with the option to visit Indore city for entertainment and shopping on weekends.

A Typical Day in a Rau Student's Life

Most students in Rau follow a fairly structured routine. Here is what a typical weekday looks like for a student living in the area:

  • 6:30 AM - 7:30 AM: Wake up, get ready, and have breakfast at your hostel or PG.
  • 8:00 AM - 1:00 PM: Morning lectures and lab sessions at college.
  • 1:00 PM - 2:00 PM: Lunch break. Head back to the hostel if it is close, or grab food at canteens nearby.
  • 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM: Afternoon classes or library time for assignments and projects.
  • 4:00 PM - 6:00 PM: Sports, gym, or socializing with friends at local hangout spots.
  • 7:00 PM - 8:00 PM: Dinner at the hostel, followed by evening walks or tea at a nearby stall.
  • 8:30 PM - 11:00 PM: Self-study, revision, and assignments. Group study sessions are common.

Students who live at hostels with meal plans, like Radiance Residency, save a lot of time since they do not have to cook or search for food. This allows them to dedicate more time to academics and extracurricular activities.

Food Scene: Where Students Eat in Rau

Rau has a surprisingly diverse food scene that caters to student budgets. You will find everything from pocket-friendly street food to decent sit-down restaurants. Popular options include Maggi and chai stalls near college gates, thali restaurants serving unlimited meals for under 100 rupees, and numerous fast food joints offering burgers, momos, and sandwiches.

For those who miss home food, several tiffin services operate in the area, delivering meals to your hostel or PG. Many students also rely on food delivery apps like Zomato and Swiggy for variety. Weekend dinners at restaurants on Bypass Road or a trip to Indore's famous Sarafa and Chappan Dukan are common student traditions.

Getting Around: Transport Options

Rau is well-connected to the rest of Indore through multiple transport options. City buses run frequently between Rau and key Indore locations like Vijay Nagar, Palasia, and Rajwada. Auto-rickshaws are available for short distances and typically charge between 20 and 50 rupees within Rau.

Many students prefer to keep a bicycle or scooty for daily commute between their hostel and college. Ride-sharing apps like Ola and Uber also operate in the area, though availability can be limited during late hours. The key advantage of living very close to your college, such as at a hostel directly opposite the campus, is that you eliminate the commute entirely and save both time and money.

Entertainment and Recreation

While Rau is primarily a student-focused area, there is no shortage of things to do after classes. Many students visit nearby gaming cafes and billiard parlours for a break. Cricket matches on local grounds are a daily evening ritual. Several gyms and fitness centres have opened near the college campuses, offering affordable student memberships.

For cinema and malls, students head to C21 Mall or Treasure Island Mall in Indore, which are around 30 to 40 minutes away by bus. Weekend trips to places like Patalpani waterfall, Ralamandal wildlife sanctuary, and Kamla Nehru Zoo are popular among students looking for a nature break. Indore itself offers vibrant nightlife, street markets, and cultural events throughout the year.

Shopping and Daily Essentials

Rau has a well-developed local market that covers all student necessities. You will find stationery shops near every college, general stores for toiletries and snacks, and medical shops for quick access to medicines. A few larger supermarkets have also come up in the area for monthly grocery shopping.

For clothes, electronics, and other bigger purchases, students typically visit Indore's commercial areas like MT Cloth Market, Topkhana, or the malls. Online shopping through Amazon and Flipkart is also widely used, with Rau having reliable delivery coverage.

Safety and Security in Rau

Safety is naturally a top concern for students and their parents. Rau is generally considered a safe area for students. The presence of multiple colleges means the local police keep a watchful eye on the neighbourhood. Most hostels and PGs in the area have CCTV cameras and security arrangements.

However, like any area, it is wise to take standard precautions. Avoid walking alone late at night in isolated areas, keep your valuables secure, and always inform your hostel warden or roommate about your whereabouts. Choosing a hostel with 24/7 security, CCTV surveillance, and controlled access, like Radiance Residency, gives both students and parents additional peace of mind.

Cost of Living for Students in Rau

One of the biggest advantages of Rau is its affordability. Here is a rough breakdown of monthly expenses for a typical student:

  • Hostel/PG (with meals): 7,000 to 12,000 rupees per month
  • Transport: 500 to 1,500 rupees (if living close to campus, nearly zero)
  • Food (outside meals): 1,000 to 3,000 rupees for snacks and outings
  • Stationery and printing: 200 to 500 rupees
  • Mobile recharge and entertainment: 500 to 1,000 rupees
  • Total estimated: 9,000 to 18,000 rupees per month
