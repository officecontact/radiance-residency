IIM Indore Area Guide — Everything Students Need to Know (2026)

Your comprehensive guide to the IIM Indore campus area — accommodation, transport, food, and student lifestyle.

March 10, 2026 9 min read Radiance Residency

The Indian Institute of Management Indore (IIM Indore) is one of India's premier business schools, attracting thousands of students and aspirants from across the country every year. Located on the Rau-Pithampur road, the IIM Indore campus sits in a rapidly developing area that offers a unique blend of academic excellence and small-town charm.

Whether you are an incoming IIM Indore student, a visiting researcher, or someone attending workshops and events on campus, this guide covers everything you need to know about the IIM Indore area in 2026.

About IIM Indore Campus

IIM Indore was established in 1996 and has since grown into one of India's top 10 management institutions. The campus is spread over 193 acres on the Rau-Pithampur road, approximately 20 kilometres from Indore city centre and about 8 kilometres from Rau town.

The campus features state-of-the-art infrastructure including modern classrooms equipped with the latest technology, a well-stocked library with digital resources, multiple auditoriums for conferences and events, sports facilities including a cricket ground, tennis courts, basketball courts, and a swimming pool, residential halls for students and faculty, a campus cafeteria and food court, and a dedicated incubation centre for startups.

The campus is known for its green, sprawling landscape and peaceful academic environment. It is designed to be self-contained, with most amenities available within the campus walls.

The Surrounding Area — Rau & Pithampur Road

The area around IIM Indore has transformed significantly over the past decade. Rau, the nearest town, has evolved from a quiet village into a bustling educational hub. Here is what you will find in the surrounding area:

  • Educational institutions: Medicaps University, IPS Academy, SD Bansal College, and IIST Indore are all located within a 5-10 km radius.
  • Commercial development: Several shopping complexes, banks (SBI, HDFC, ICICI), ATMs, and retail stores have come up along the Rau main road.
  • Healthcare: Multiple clinics and pharmacies are available in Rau. For major hospitals, Bombay Hospital and MY Hospital in Indore city are accessible within 30-40 minutes.
  • Recreation: Movie theatres, parks, and entertainment zones are available both in Rau and in the main Indore city area.

Accommodation Options Near IIM Indore

While IIM Indore provides on-campus hostels for its regular students, there are several situations where off-campus accommodation becomes necessary — for visiting faculty, short-course participants, family members visiting students, or students from nearby colleges who want to be in the IIM area.

On-Campus Hostels

IIM Indore provides furnished hostel rooms for all PGP (Post Graduate Programme) students. The rooms are single-occupancy with attached bathrooms. Campus hostels include laundry, mess facilities, common rooms with TV, and 24/7 Wi-Fi. However, these are reserved exclusively for enrolled IIM students.

Off-Campus Options in Rau

For those who need accommodation near the IIM Indore area but are not enrolled IIM students, Rau offers multiple options. PG (Paying Guest) accommodations are available starting from ₹5,000-₹8,000 per month. Private hostels like Radiance Residency provide fully furnished rooms with meals and amenities included. Rental apartments are also available in the Rau area, though these tend to be more expensive and require longer commitments.

Transport & Connectivity

Getting to and from IIM Indore is straightforward, though having your own transport can make life significantly easier. Here are the main transport options:

  • Auto-rickshaws: Available from Rau to IIM Indore campus. Expect to pay ₹30-50 for a shared auto and ₹100-150 for a private auto.
  • City buses: AICTSL city buses connect Rau to main Indore. Routes pass through the IIM area. Bus fare is ₹10-20.
  • Ride-hailing apps: Ola and Uber are available in the area, though availability can be limited during off-peak hours. Rau to Indore city centre costs approximately ₹200-300.
  • Two-wheelers: Many students rent bikes or scooters for ₹2,500-4,000 per month. This is the most flexible option for getting around.
  • Nearest railway station: Indore Junction (20 km from IIM Indore). Rau also has a small railway station.
  • Nearest airport: Devi Ahilyabai Holkar Airport (approximately 25 km from IIM Indore). Taxis from the airport to IIM Indore cost ₹500-700.

Food & Dining Options

While IIM Indore has its own mess and cafeteria, the surrounding area offers diverse dining choices for different budgets and tastes:

  • Street food: Rau's main market has excellent street food options — poha, jalebi, samosa, kachori, and the famous Indori chaat. Prices range from ₹20-60 per plate.
  • Budget restaurants: Several affordable thali restaurants in Rau offer full meals for ₹80-120. Popular spots include Rajasthani Bhojnalaya and Shree Krishna Restaurant.
  • Cafes: Multiple student-friendly cafes have opened along the Rau-Pithampur road, including chai stalls and modern coffee houses. Chai Sutta Bar, which actually originated in Indore, has an outlet near Rau.
  • Food delivery: Swiggy and Zomato deliver to the IIM Indore area, though delivery times can be longer compared to main Indore city.
  • Hostel meals: Students living at hostels like Radiance Residency get three home-style vegetarian meals daily included in their rent.

Student Lifestyle & Recreation

Life around IIM Indore is a blend of academic intensity and laid-back small-town living. Here is what the lifestyle looks like for students in the area:

Weekdays are typically filled with classes, assignments, and case study preparations. Most students stick to the campus or their nearby accommodation. The IIM campus library is open late, and many students prefer studying there.

Weekends see students heading into main Indore for shopping at malls like C21, Treasure Island, or Phoenix Citadel. The Sarafa Bazaar night food market is a must-visit experience, open from 8 PM onwards with legendary street food. Students also visit Rajwada Palace, Lal Bagh Palace, and other heritage sites.

Fitness enthusiasts can use the IIM campus sports facilities (for enrolled students) or join one of the several gyms in Rau. Morning and evening walks along the Rau-Pithampur road are popular among students.

Climate in the area is typical of central India — hot summers (March-June, up to 45 degrees Celsius), a pleasant monsoon season (July-September), and mild winters (November-February). Students should plan their wardrobe and accommodation (AC vs Non-AC) accordingly.

Essential Services & Utilities

Here is a quick rundown of essential services available in the IIM Indore area:

  • Banking: SBI, HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, and Bank of Baroda branches and ATMs are available in Rau.
  • Mobile networks: Jio, Airtel, and Vi all have good coverage in the area. Jio tends to have the best data speeds.
  • Stationery & printing: Multiple photocopy and stationery shops are available near the colleges in Rau.
  • Groceries: Local kirana stores and small supermarkets in Rau cover daily needs. For larger shopping, DMart and Big Bazaar in Indore are 20-30 minutes away.
  • Laundry: Several affordable laundry services operate in the Rau area, charging ₹30-50 per kg for wash and fold.
