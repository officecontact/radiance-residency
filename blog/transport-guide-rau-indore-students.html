Transport Guide for Students in Rau & Indore — Buses, Autos & More (2026)

Everything you need to know about getting around Rau and Indore as a student — routes, costs, and money-saving tips.

March 20, 2026 9 min read Radiance Residency

One of the first challenges new students face when they move to Rau or Indore for college is figuring out how to get around. Unlike major metros with extensive metro rail networks, Indore relies on a mix of city buses, auto-rickshaws, shared tempos, and ride-hailing apps. Understanding these options will save you both time and money throughout your college years.

This guide covers all the transport options available to students in the Rau-Indore area in 2026, complete with routes, approximate costs, and practical tips.

1. AICTSL City Buses — The Cheapest Option

Atal Indore City Transport Services Limited (AICTSL) operates a network of city buses that connect Rau to various parts of Indore. These are by far the cheapest way to travel.

Fare: ₹10-25 depending on distance. Students can get a monthly pass for approximately ₹300-500, which allows unlimited travel on specific routes.

Key routes for students in Rau:

  • Rau to Rajwada (City Centre): Multiple buses available. Journey time approximately 40-50 minutes. Buses run every 15-20 minutes during peak hours.
  • Rau to Vijay Nagar: Direct buses available. Journey time approximately 30-40 minutes. Vijay Nagar is where many students go for shopping, cafes, and entertainment.
  • Rau to Indore Junction Railway Station: Journey time approximately 45-60 minutes. Useful for students travelling home by train.
  • Rau to Sarwate Bus Stand: The main inter-city bus terminal. Journey time approximately 40-50 minutes.

Tips for bus travel: The buses tend to be crowded during morning (8-10 AM) and evening (5-7 PM) rush hours. Try to travel during off-peak hours when possible. The AICTSL buses are generally clean and well-maintained. Use the "Indore City Bus" app or Google Maps for real-time bus tracking and route planning.

2. Auto-Rickshaws — The Flexible Choice

Auto-rickshaws are the most flexible transport option in Rau and Indore. They are available as both shared and private rides.

Shared Autos

Shared autos run on fixed routes and are significantly cheaper than private autos. You share the ride with other passengers going in the same direction.

  • Rau to Pigdamber: ₹10-15
  • Rau Square to Bypass Road: ₹15-20
  • Rau to Simrol/IIM Indore area: ₹20-30
  • Rau to Vijay Nagar: ₹30-40

Private Autos

Private autos take you door-to-door but cost more. Always negotiate the fare before getting in, as most auto-rickshaws in the Rau-Indore area do not use meters.

  • Within Rau: ₹30-80 depending on distance
  • Rau to Indore city centre: ₹150-250
  • Rau to Airport: ₹350-500
  • Rau to Railway Station: ₹200-350

Pro tip: Always agree on the fare before sitting in the auto. Auto drivers in the Rau area generally quote fair prices, but it is still a good practice to confirm. If you are new, ask a local student or your hostel staff about the standard fare for your route before heading out.

3. Ola & Uber — App-Based Rides

Both Ola and Uber operate in the Indore area, though availability in Rau can be limited compared to main Indore city, especially during late nights and early mornings.

Approximate costs:

  • Rau to Indore city centre (auto): ₹120-180
  • Rau to Indore city centre (cab): ₹200-300
  • Rau to Airport: ₹400-600
  • Rau to Railway Station: ₹250-400

Money-saving tips for ride-hailing: Use Ola Share or Uber Pool for cheaper shared rides when available. Keep an eye on both apps and compare prices before booking — one is often cheaper than the other. Late-night surge pricing can be significant, so plan your return trips from the city early. Many students pool money with 2-3 friends and share a cab ride, effectively splitting the cost.

4. Two-Wheeler Rentals — Freedom on Wheels

Many students in Rau opt to rent a bike or scooter for the duration of their stay. This gives maximum flexibility and often works out cheaper than daily auto/cab rides if you travel frequently.

  • Scooter (Activa, Access): ₹2,500-3,500/month
  • Bike (Splendor, Pulsar): ₹3,000-4,500/month
  • Security deposit: ₹3,000-5,000 (refundable)
  • Fuel costs: Budget approximately ₹800-1,200/month depending on usage

Several rental shops operate near Rau Square and along the bypass road. Ask your hostel staff for recommendations for reliable rental providers. You will need a valid driving licence, Aadhaar card, and one passport-size photograph to rent. Some apps like Bounce and Vogo also offer short-term bike rentals in the Indore area.

Important: Always wear a helmet. Traffic police in Indore are strict about helmet enforcement, and the fine for riding without a helmet is ₹1,000. Also, make sure you have valid insurance for any rented vehicle.

5. Walking — The Underrated Option

Do not underestimate the power of walking, especially if you live close to your college. Many students in the Rau area are within walking distance (10-20 minutes) of their colleges, and choosing to walk not only saves money but also keeps you fit.

For example, students at Radiance Residency can walk to Medicaps University in just 2 minutes — it is literally across the road. This eliminates transport costs entirely for the daily college commute, saving you ₹1,500-3,000 per month compared to students who take autos from further away. Over an academic year, that adds up to ₹15,000-30,000 in savings.

6. Long-Distance Travel from Rau

When you need to travel home for holidays or attend events in other cities, here are your options:

By Train

Indore Junction is the main railway station, about 20 km from Rau. Well-connected to major cities like Bhopal (5-6 hours), Mumbai (12-14 hours), Delhi (14-16 hours), and Ahmedabad (8-10 hours). Book tickets through the IRCTC app or website. Tatkal booking opens at 10 AM (AC classes) and 11 AM (sleeper class) one day before the journey. Always book early during holiday season, as trains to major cities fill up weeks in advance.

By Bus

Sarwate Bus Stand is the main inter-city bus terminal. MPSTRC (government) and private operators run services to Bhopal (4-5 hours, ₹300-600), Ujjain (2 hours, ₹100-200), Dewas (1 hour, ₹80-150), and Mumbai (10-12 hours, ₹600-1,500). Private buses can be booked through RedBus, AbhiBus, or MakeMyTrip. Sleeper buses are comfortable for overnight journeys and are often cheaper than trains.

By Air

Devi Ahilyabai Holkar Airport is about 25 km from Rau. Direct flights are available to Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Kolkata, and several other cities. Budget airlines like IndiGo, SpiceJet, and AirAsia offer student-friendly fares if booked 2-3 weeks in advance. A cab from Rau to the airport costs ₹400-600.

Monthly Transport Budget for Students

Here is a realistic monthly transport budget breakdown for students in the Rau area:

  • If you live near your college (like Radiance Residency near Medicaps): ₹500-1,000/month (occasional trips to city)
  • If you take shared autos daily: ₹1,500-2,500/month
  • If you rent a two-wheeler: ₹3,500-5,000/month (rental + fuel)
  • If you rely on Ola/Uber: ₹3,000-5,000/month
